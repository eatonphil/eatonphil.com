Phil Eaton

Hey! I'm Phil (he/him). In the past I've been a developer, a manager, and a cofounder. At companies as large as Oracle and as small as TigerBeetle. Today I'm a staff engineer working on Postgres products at EnterpriseDB.

I am a college dropout (not by choice; stay in school!) and for the last 10 years I've chased my way down the software stack starting from humble beginnings with the venerable jQuery and PHP.

I try to practice healthy obsession.

To that end, I run the NYC Systems Coffee Club (500+ members), co-run the NYC Systems talk series (1k+ members), run an online Software Internals Book Club (2k+ members), run /r/databasedevelopment (10k+ members), and I run a Software Internals Discord (9k+ members) that has, among other things, become a home for database and distributed systems developers.

I play a good deal of volleyball in NYC, keep trying to cook new dishes, love to read, started running, and occasionally play music.

My writing, which in 2024 passed 1 million total views, has been cited in the book Writing for Developers.

I hope you connect with me on LinkedIn (9k+ followers), Twitter (26k+ followers), Bluesky (6k+ followers), my mailing list (3k+ subscribers), or over email.

I absolutely love to meet people. If you ever want to catch up, here's a little page to help me out with scheduling.
