title = NYC Systems --- ### What?

A series of tech talks around systems programming.

Developers sharing their experience building, debugging, testing, and maintaining:

  • Compilers, parsers, virtual machines, IDEs, profiling, etc.
  • Databases, storage, networking, distributed systems
  • Large scale infrastructure, low latency, high availability services
  • Formal methods, verification
  • Browsers, kernel development, security, etc.

### For potential venues

Thank you for your interest! The ideal venue will seat 50-75. We can reasonably expect at least 20 attendees per event. We need only doors to open at 6PM and we can be out by 8PM.

For the venue, this is a good opportunity to get in front of smart systems developers at startups and enterprise companies. The venue is welcome to make an announcement before each event, such as a hiring call.

Similarly this series will provide a unique educational experience for your employees.

### For potential speakers

Thank your for your interest! It would be excellent to work together. Please read on so we are on the same page about what we are looking for.

Talks should start broad but go deep, taking a smart audience on an interesting journey into the challenges of some particular topic they may not have a background in. Talks should not stay high-level only for the sake of babying the audience.

Talks should not be product pitches. It is ok for speakers to mention their company but the talks should highlight and show off their engineering organization, practices, and challenges; not their product.

Speakers can expect an audience of talented developers across a wide variety of systems fields. These talks are a good opportunity to get engineers excited about the challenges the speaker's organization face. That is, speakers are always welcome to mention if they are hiring.
