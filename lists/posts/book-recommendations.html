title = General book recommendations date = January 4, 2023 ---

When looking at book recommendations it's often as useful to know what was not included as what was. I've read 275 books since I started keeping track in 2016. These 16 books I easily recommend, especially to professionals in tech.

Mastering prose

  • Wind, Sand and Stars by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
  • A Moveable Feast by Ernest Hemingway
  • West with the Night by Beryl Markham
  • A Connecticut Yankee in King Arthur's Court by Mark Twain
  • On Writing Well by William Zinsser

Understanding where we are today

  • Master of the Senate by Robert Caro
  • Postwar: A History of Europe Since 1945 by Tony Judt
  • Stocks for the Long Run by Jeremy Siegel
  • The Last Lion by William Manchester

Humor in science fiction and fantasy

  • The Cyberiad by Stanisław Lem
  • Mort by Terry Pratchett
  • The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy by Douglas Adams
  • The Sirens of Titan, and Cat's Cradle by Kurt Vonnegut

Uncategorically enjoyable

  • Hard to Be a God by Arkady Strugatsky and Boris Strugatsky.
  • Foundation by Isaac Asimov

Conspicuous books not on the list

  • The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman
  • Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman

    Notes: The problem is that much of the research wasn't reproducible.
